1. Purpose

This memo establishes clear guidelines on when and how Value Added Tax (VAT) must be charged to clients of Upali Immigration Service Consultancy. It applies to all immigration service packages, including the standard AED 15,000 all-inclusive package covering consultancy for migration to Europe, New Zealand, and Canada.

4. VAT Charging Rules by Client Type

4.1 UAE-Resident Clients (Inside UAE) — Charge 5% VAT on Consultancy Fee

Legal Basis: Article 29, UAE VAT Law — place of supply is UAE; standard rate applies.

  • Government fees (visa fee, embassy appointment fee) are out of scope — disbursements paid on behalf of the client.
  • International air tickets are zero-rated under Article 45(3), UAE VAT Law.

Sample Tax Invoice — UAE Resident Client:

INVOICE TO: [UAE Resident Client Name] Consultancy Fee AED 8,000.00 VAT @ 5% on Consultancy Fee AED 400.00 ────────────────────────────────────────────────── Visa Application Fee AED 3,000.00 (Disbursement – No VAT) Embassy Appointment Fee AED 1,000.00 (Disbursement – No VAT) International Air Ticket AED 3,000.00 (Zero-Rated – VAT 0%) ────────────────────────────────────────────────── TOTAL DUE AED 15,400.00

4.2 Outside-UAE Clients (Non-UAE Residents) — Zero-Rate the Consultancy Fee

Legal Basis: Article 31, UAE VAT Executive Regulations — export of services is zero-rated when all four conditions are met:

  1. The customer has a place of residence outside the UAE.
  2. The customer does not have a place of establishment or fixed establishment in the UAE.
  3. The customer is outside the UAE at the time the services are performed.
  4. The service is not directly related to real estate or movable assets located in the UAE.
Risk: If the client visits UAE at any point during the service period (e.g., biometric appointment), the supply may become standard-rated at 5%. Reassess before invoicing.

Sample Invoice — Outside-UAE Client (Zero-Rated):

INVOICE TO: [Foreign Client Name – Country of Residence] Consultancy Fee AED 8,000.00 (Zero-Rated – Export of Services) VAT AED 0.00 ────────────────────────────────────────────────── Visa Application Fee AED 3,000.00 (Disbursement – No VAT) Embassy Appointment Fee AED 1,000.00 (Disbursement – No VAT) International Air Ticket AED 3,000.00 (Zero-Rated – VAT 0%) ────────────────────────────────────────────────── TOTAL DUE AED 15,000.00 Zero-rating applied pursuant to Article 31, UAE VAT Executive Regulations.

5. Mandatory Documentation for Zero-Rating (Outside-UAE Clients)

Collect and file all four documents before issuing a zero-rated invoice:

  • Copy of valid foreign passport (showing no UAE residency visa)
  • Proof of foreign residential address (utility bill, bank statement — not older than 3 months)
  • Signed service agreement stating the client's domicile is outside the UAE
  • Written declaration confirming client did not enter UAE during the service period
Retention: All documents must be kept for a minimum of 5 years (Article 78, UAE VAT Law).

6. Invoice Requirements (Article 59, UAE VAT Executive Regulations)

  • The words "Tax Invoice" on the document
  • Company name, address, and Tax Registration Number (TRN) once registered
  • Client's name and address
  • Invoice date and unique sequential invoice number
  • Description of each supply with quantity and unit price
  • VAT rate applied to each line item and the VAT amount in AED
  • Total amount payable in AED
  • For zero-rated supplies: state reason — "Zero-Rated Export of Services – Article 31"

9. Billing Team Action Checklist (Per Invoice)

  • Identify client type: UAE resident OR outside-UAE resident
  • If outside-UAE: collect all 4 mandatory documents (Section 5) before zero-rating
  • Break down the AED 15,000 package into its 4 components on the invoice
  • Apply correct VAT rate to each component (refer to Section 4)
  • Ensure Tax Invoice contains all required fields (Section 6)
  • File client documents in compliance records folder
  • Update monthly turnover tracker
