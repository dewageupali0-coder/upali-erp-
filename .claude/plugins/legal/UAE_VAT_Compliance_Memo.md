## 1. PURPOSE

This memo establishes clear guidelines on when and how Value Added Tax (VAT) must be charged to clients of Upali Immigration Service Consultancy. It applies to all immigration service packages offered by the company, including the standard AED 15,000 all-inclusive package covering consultancy to Europe, New Zealand, and Canada.

## 4. VAT CHARGING RULES BY CLIENT TYPE

### 4.1 UAE-Resident Clients (Inside UAE)

**Rule: Charge 5% VAT on the Consultancy Fee component only.**

- Legal Basis: Article 29, UAE VAT Law — place of supply is UAE; standard rate applies.
- Government fees (visa fee, embassy appointment fee) are **out of scope** — they are disbursements paid on behalf of the client and must not carry VAT.
- International air tickets are **zero-rated** under Article 45(3), UAE VAT Law.

**Sample Tax Invoice — UAE Resident Client:**

```
INVOICE TO: [UAE Resident Client Name]

  Consultancy Fee                AED  8,000.00
  VAT @ 5% on Consultancy Fee   AED    400.00
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────
  Visa Application Fee           AED  3,000.00   (Disbursement – No VAT)
  Embassy Appointment Fee        AED  1,000.00   (Disbursement – No VAT)
  International Air Ticket       AED  3,000.00   (Zero-Rated – VAT 0%)
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────
  TOTAL DUE                      AED 15,400.00
```

---

### 4.2 Outside-UAE Clients (Non-UAE Residents)

**Rule: Zero-rate the Consultancy Fee as an export of services (0% VAT), subject to documented conditions being met.**

- Legal Basis: Article 31, UAE VAT Executive Regulations — export of services is zero-rated when all four conditions are satisfied:

  1. The customer has a place of residence **outside the UAE**.
  2. The customer does **not** have a place of establishment or fixed establishment in the UAE.
  3. The customer is **outside the UAE** at the time the services are performed.
  4. The service is **not directly related** to real estate or movable assets located in the UAE.

- If **all four conditions are met**, issue a zero-rated invoice.
- If the client **visits UAE** at any point during the service period, the supply may become standard-rated (5%). Reassess before invoicing.

**Sample Invoice — Outside-UAE Client (Zero-Rated):**

```
INVOICE TO: [Foreign Client Name – Country of Residence]

  Consultancy Fee                AED  8,000.00   (Zero-Rated – Export of Services)
  VAT                            AED      0.00
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────
  Visa Application Fee           AED  3,000.00   (Disbursement – No VAT)
  Embassy Appointment Fee        AED  1,000.00   (Disbursement – No VAT)
  International Air Ticket       AED  3,000.00   (Zero-Rated – VAT 0%)
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────
  TOTAL DUE                      AED 15,000.00

  Zero-rating applied pursuant to Article 31, UAE VAT Executive Regulations.
```

## 5. MANDATORY DOCUMENTATION FOR ZERO-RATING (OUTSIDE-UAE CLIENTS)

Before issuing a zero-rated invoice, the billing team **must** collect and file the following documents for each outside-UAE client:

- [ ] Copy of valid foreign passport (showing no UAE residency visa)
- [ ] Proof of foreign residential address (utility bill, bank statement, or tenancy agreement — not older than 3 months)
- [ ] Signed service agreement stating the client's domicile is outside the UAE
- [ ] Confirmation that the client did not enter the UAE during the service period (email or written declaration)

> All documentation must be retained for a minimum of **5 years** in accordance with Article 78, UAE VAT Law.

---

## 6. INVOICING REQUIREMENTS

All invoices issued must comply with UAE Tax Invoice requirements (Article 59, UAE VAT Executive Regulations):

1. The words **"Tax Invoice"** must appear on the document.
2. Company name, address, and **Tax Registration Number (TRN)** (once registered).
3. Client's name and address.
4. Invoice date and a unique sequential invoice number.
5. Description of each supply with quantity and unit price.
6. The **VAT rate applied to each line item** and the VAT amount in AED.
7. Total amount payable in AED.
8. For zero-rated supplies: state the reason (e.g., "Zero-Rated Export of Services – Article 31").

> **Never issue a single-line invoice for AED 15,000.** Bundled invoices expose the company to FTA penalties for incorrect VAT treatment.

## 8. KEY COMPLIANCE RISKS & MITIGATIONS

| Risk | Consequence | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Charging VAT to outside-UAE clients without zero-rating | Client overcharged; reputational risk | Apply zero-rating with documented proof |
| Not charging VAT to UAE-resident clients | FTA penalty + back-payment of VAT + surcharge | Always charge 5% on consultancy fee for UAE residents |
| Issuing bundled (single-line) invoices | Incorrect VAT declared; FTA audit exposure | Itemize every invoice into 4 components |
| Failing to register before threshold | AED 20,000 penalty | Monthly turnover tracking |
| Poor record-keeping | FTA audit failure | Retain all records for 5 years minimum |
| Misunderstanding Free Zone exemption | Non-compliance with VAT Law | Free Zone License ≠ VAT exemption for services |
